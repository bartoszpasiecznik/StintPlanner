# Product

## Register

product

## Users

LMU Stint Planner is for Le Mans Ultimate endurance teams, race strategists, stint planners, and team coordinators who need to prepare and adjust race plans around driver rotation, stint limits, tyres, pit timing, VE, fuel, and driver availability windows. Users are often planning under time pressure before or during organized team races, where mistakes in a stint or stop plan can create avoidable race problems.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to turn a complex endurance race setup into a readable, editable stint plan. Success means the planner can quickly understand the race length, total laps, driver sequence, pit-stop timing, tyre-set usage, VE or fuel requirements, warnings, and projected finish without losing sight of the mixed stint and pit timeline.

## Brand Personality

Motorsport command center: focused, technical, purposeful, and race-aware. The interface should feel like a serious planning console for endurance racing rather than a generic office tool, while keeping controls familiar and readable enough for repeated operational use.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboard patterns: oversized metric cards, decorative gradients, marketing-like spacing, vague productivity-tool styling, and repeated same-looking cards that do not clarify the race plan. Also avoid reverting the planner into a spreadsheet-first interface or turning it into a decorative game HUD that competes with the data.

## Design Principles

1. Put the race plan first: the generated stint and pit timeline should be the clearest object on the screen.
2. Preserve expert density: compact controls and cards are valuable when they reduce scanning effort without hiding important state.
3. Make resource pressure visible: tyres, VE, fuel, driver caps, and warnings should surface as operational constraints, not buried settings.
4. Keep editing predictable: controls should use familiar desktop patterns and avoid surprising interaction models.
5. Let motorsport tone support the task: racing atmosphere is welcome only when it improves hierarchy, orientation, or confidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA behavior for a desktop planning tool: strong text contrast, visible keyboard focus, color-blind-safe status cues that do not rely on color alone, readable compact controls, sufficient touch or pointer targets where practical, and reduced-motion-friendly transitions if motion is introduced.
